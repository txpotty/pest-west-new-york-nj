Reliable and Trustworthy Common Problems and Signs You Need Wildlife Control

Recognizing the signs of a wildlife problem early can prevent significant damage and stress. If you notice any of the following issues, it's a strong indicator that you may need professional wildlife control services in West New York, NJ:

Strange Noises

Hearing scratching, rustling, thumping, or chattering sounds coming from your attic, walls, crawl space, or chimney, especially at night or in the early morning, can be a sign of animal activity. Different animals make different noises. Squirrels often scurry and scratch, while raccoons might make louder thumping or chattering sounds. Ignoring these noises allows the problem to escalate.

These noises frequently indicate that animals are nesting, foraging, or moving around within concealed areas of your structure. The sounds can vary based on the type of animal and its activity level. Early detection through sound recognition is key to addressing the issue before it causes extensive damage or creates a larger infestation problem. Our trained technicians are often able to identify the type of animal present based on the sounds they hear, helping us to formulate a targeted plan for removal and exclusion.

Visible Damage to Property

Wildlife can cause a variety of physical damage to your home or business. This can include chewed electrical wires, damaged insulation, torn screens, gnawed wood, or holes in your roof or siding. These damages are not only unsightly but can also compromise the structural integrity of your building and create potential fire hazards.

The extent of the damage can range from minor cosmetic issues to significant structural problems. For example, rodents like mice and rats chewing through electrical wiring is a serious fire risk. Animals nesting in attics can compact and damage insulation, leading to increased energy costs. Identifying and repairing this damage is an integral part of our wildlife control process, ensuring the long-term protection of your property and preventing further access for other animals.

Unpleasant Odors

The presence of wildlife often leads to unpleasant odors caused by urine, droppings, and decaying carcasses. These smells can be strong and persistent, particularly in enclosed spaces like attics or crawl spaces. Ignoring these odors can lead to health issues and make your living or working environment uncomfortable.

Animal waste can contain harmful bacteria and pathogens that can affect air quality and pose health risks to occupants. The odors can also attract other pests, exacerbating the problem. Our sanitation services are designed to thoroughly clean and disinfect affected areas, neutralizing odors and creating a healthier environment. Addressing the source of the odor is crucial for a complete and effective wildlife control solution, ensuring your property is free from unpleasant smells and potential health hazards after the animal has been removed.

Animal Sightings

Actually seeing wildlife on your property, especially during the daytime or in unusual locations (like inside your home), is a clear sign of a potential infestation. While some wildlife sightings might be transient, persistent sightings or animals acting unafraid of humans can indicate a deeper issue.

Seeing animals on your porch, in your yard, or attempting to enter your home can be unsettling. It suggests that the animal has become comfortable in the area and may have established a den or nesting site nearby. Pet Behavior Changes

If your pets are exhibiting unusual behavior, such as barking excessively at walls, scratching at certain areas, or becoming agitated, it could be a sign that they sense wildlife within your home. Animals have keen senses and can often detect the presence of other animals before humans do.

Pets' reactions can be a valuable early warning system for wildlife intrusions. Their agitation might be directed towards the specific location where the animal is hiding. Paying attention to your pet's behavior and investigating the areas they are focusing on can help you pinpoint the source of the problem before it becomes more apparent. This is particularly true for animals that are nesting in attics or crawl spaces, making them difficult for humans to detect initially but easily sensed by a pet's acute hearing and smell.

Evidence of Droppings or Nesting Materials

Discovering animal droppings, urine stains, or nesting materials (such as leaves, twigs, or shredded insulation) in your attic, garage, or outbuildings is a definitive sign of wildlife infestation. The appearance of droppings can often help identify the type of animal involved.

Animal waste poses health risks and can attract other pests. The presence of nesting materials indicates that the animal is likely living and potentially breeding on your property. Prompt removal of these materials and thorough cleaning are essential to mitigate health risks and prevent further infestation. Identifying the specific type of droppings or nesting material is a crucial step our technicians take during the initial assessment to determine the target animal for removal and tailor the most effective treatment plan.

Are you also seeing unusual spider activity? An increase in spider populations could be linked to an increased food source provided by another pest infestation, potentially brought in by wildlife.

Damaged Gardens or Landscaping

Certain wildlife, such as groundhogs, rabbits, or deer, can cause damage to your gardens, landscaping, or vegetable patches. This can include eaten plants, damaged bark on trees, or burrow holes in your yard. While some garden damage is unavoidable, significant or persistent damage may indicate a larger wildlife issue.

Animals foraging in your garden not only ruin your plants but can also create pathways or attract other animals seeking food. Identifying the type of damage can help determine the animal responsible and guide the appropriate control measures. Our wildlife control services can help protect your landscaping investments and prevent further damage by implementing effective exclusion methods and removal strategies tailored to the specific animal causing the problem. Consider if you might need termite treatment if you notice damage to wooden structures near your garden, as some wildlife can exacerbate existing pest problems.

If you have noticed an increase in flying insects around your property, such as bees or wasps, it's important to address those as well. Bee and wasp removal can be necessary if nests are located close to areas where wildlife activity is present, as both can pose risks to your property and safety.

Disturbed Trash Cans

Overturned trash cans, scattered garbage, and damaged bins are common signs of animals like raccoons or bears foraging for food. These animals are attracted to accessible food sources and can cause a mess while searching for a meal. This not only creates an unsightly situation but can also attract more pests.

Securing your trash cans and removing attractants are important preventative measures, but persistent scavenging is often a sign of a larger wildlife population in the area or an animal that has become habituated to finding food on your property. Our wildlife control services can help address the root cause of this behavior and implement strategies to deter animals from your property, such as recommendations for proper waste management and exclusion techniques around potential food sources. If you're also dealing with other pests attracted to the garbage, like cockroaches, addressing the wildlife issue is a crucial first step in comprehensive pest management.

Another common problem that can occur concurrently with wildlife issues is an infestation of bed bugs. While not directly related to wildlife, a wildlife problem can create conditions that make your home more vulnerable to other pest issues, highlighting the importance of a holistic approach to pest management.

Presence of Burrows or Nests

Discovering burrows in your yard or finding nests in trees, chimneys, or attics confirms the presence of wildlife. Burrows can damage your lawn and create trip hazards, while nests inside structures can pose fire risks and create sanitation problems.

The presence of a burrow or nest indicates that the animal has established a more permanent presence on your property. Locating and safely removing these nesting sites is essential for effective wildlife control. Our technicians are trained to identify and safely remove various types of burrows and nests, minimizing disruption to your property while ensuring the humane removal of the animals. This is crucial for preventing the animals from raising their young on your property and establishing a long-term presence. You might also be noticing signs of other pests like mosquitoes breeding in standing water caused by improper drainage or holes created by wildlife.

Damage to Electrical Wiring or Plumbing

Some wildlife, particularly rodents and squirrels, are known to chew on electrical wiring and plumbing pipes. This can lead to power outages, water leaks, and even fire hazards. Damage to these essential systems is a serious issue that requires immediate attention.

Animals chew on wires for various reasons, including sharpening their teeth or using the material for nesting. Damaged wiring is a significant fire risk, and damaged plumbing can lead to costly water damage. Addressing wildlife problems that involve damage to these systems is critical for the safety and integrity of your home. Our services include inspecting for such damage and providing recommendations for necessary repairs after the animals have been removed. Signs of Disease or Parasites

Wildlife can carry diseases and parasites that can be transmitted to humans and pets. If you notice sick-looking animals, excessive fleas, or ticks on your property, it could be related to wildlife activity. This highlights the health risks associated with wildlife infestations.

Animals like raccoons and squirrels can carry rabies, distemper, and other diseases. They can also introduce parasites like fleas and ticks into your home, which can then infest your pets. If you suspect wildlife on your property is sick or heavily infested with parasites, it's essential to avoid direct contact and contact professional wildlife control immediately. Our services include recommendations for addressing secondary infestations like tick control that might be linked to the wildlife problem.

If you encounter aggressive wildlife on your property, it's important to maintain a safe distance and contact professionals for assistance.

Aggressive wildlife can pose a direct threat to the safety of you, your family, and your pets. Attempting to handle aggressive animals yourself is dangerous. Our trained technicians have the knowledge and experience to safely and humanely handle aggressive wildlife situations, minimizing risk and ensuring the safety of everyone involved.
